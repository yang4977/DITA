  • Configuring Basic Exposure Settings

    Manual mode consists of three beasic exposure settings: ISO, Aperture, and Shutter Speed

    • Adjusting the ISO

      By adjusting the ISO, the camera sensor becomes more or less sensitive to light. DSLRs typically have an ISO range of 100 (low sensitivity) to 12,800 (high sensitivity). A high ISO will create a brighter photograph, with a grainer image. A low ISO will yield a sharper and darker image.

    • Modifying the Aperture

      By modifying the aperture, you are widening or narrowing the camera lens. The aperture is measured by the f-stop. A higher f-stop (near f/2.8) will allow for a narrower lens and will create a darker image. A lower f-stop (near f/16) will allow a wider lens opening with more depth to the image.
